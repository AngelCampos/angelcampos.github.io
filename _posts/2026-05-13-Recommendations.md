Here are some recommendations of tools I use almost, if not, every day to make my work easier
and fun. Hope you find it useful and drop a line below if you end up using them! :D

# Tools

## KanbanFlow

Tasks and time management. [KanbanFlow](https://kanbanflow.com/){:target="_blank"} Is a project
management tool that integrates both the [Kanban](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kanban_(development)){:target="_blank"}
tasks management method, and the 
[Pomodoro technique](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pomodoro_Technique){:target="_blank"}.

This app helps me DAILY to keep focus, organize tasks, track progress, record my effort, and 
importantly, also reminding me to take breaks and stretch :) .

I color-code and sort my tasks by priority
{<span style="color:lightblue">low</span>.
<span style="color:green">Medium</span>.
<span style="color:orange">High</span>.
<span style="color:red">Highest</span>.}, then pull them to a column based on the 
date I intend for the task to be completed on {Backlog, This month, This week, Today, Done}.

I recommend starting as simply as possible, making it a habit, and then making it your
own by further customizing the board. 

## Paperpile

As Schargi once said, "The most useful piece of software I have ever
encountered". 
[Paperpile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paperpile){:target="_blank"} 
is a Reference Manager that has worked very well for me.
It is highly integrated with Google Scholar and Google Chrome, which makes it
very easy to add papers automatically using a provided proxy. It works very
well with Google Docs, which has been part of our writing workflow, and Word
as well. Organizing and exporting BIB files is also very easy and has some
integrated workflows for this as well, to automatically update BIB files in
Google Drive (which can be used for Obsidian notes, discussed below).

As of recently, I am starting to use it to keep my notes in one place and

## Duckduckgo Bangs

I've been using the [Duckduckgo](https://duckduckgo.com/){:target="_blank"}
(DDG) search engine for many years now. For me, it's greatest feature may be
for others its greatest flaw, it is not personalized search, as it doesn't use cookies to track you.
Trying to avoid the [filter bubble effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Filter_bubble){:target="_blank"} 
is the reason why I prefer this approach. 

But the most important reason I've been keeping DDG as my default search
engine is its [Bangs!](https://duckduckgo.com/bangs){:target="_blank"}, 
shortcuts that quickly take you to other search engines
or sites. For example, typing:

- ```!w Popocatepetl```: Will take you to the Wikipedia article about the
  Popocatepetl volcano, which happens to be pictured on my website's homepage
  (My best picture probably).
- ```!pubmed Schwartz Schraga```: Will take you to PubMed listing all papers
  authored by Schragi (My PhD PI).
- ```!gsc Pan-mod-seq```: Will take you to Google Scholar search
  for "Pan-mod-seq" (which is clearly my latest paper :P).

And so on and so on, as [Zizek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slavoj_%C5%BDi%C5%BEek){:target="_blank"}
would say, hehe.

For all Bangs look on [DDGs website](https://duckduckgo.com/bangs){:target="_blank"}
